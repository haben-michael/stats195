[from bulletin] This short course runs for the first four weeks of the quarter and is offered in fall and spring. It is recommended for students who want to use R in statistics, science, or engineering courses and for students who want to learn the basics of R programming. The goal of the short course is to familiarize students with R's tools for scientific computing. Lectures will be interactive with a focus on learning by example, and assignments will be application-driven. No prior programming experience is needed. Intro slides.

Requirements: 3 short (less than 3hrs) programming-based assignments, each receiving a grade of at least 60%.

Similar courses: If you want a short intro to R from the ground up, you could try my 2-hr lecture in the medical school (use the "picture in picture" link; the flyer is here and the script to follow along is here).

If you want an introduction to R that assumes prior programming experience, you could try CS109L. Other courses in the bulletin offering to teach R: BIOS 205: Introduction to R; POLISCI 150B: Machine Learning for Social Scientists (POLISCI 355B); BIO 202: Ecological Statistics.
